欧盟对BRI的回应：政策演变

The above cases reveal that the EU's response to the BRI has evolved dynamically, seeking coordination amid competition. This section traces the trajectory from passive observation to active "de-risking." 上述案例揭示了欧盟对BRI的回应是动态演变的，在竞争中寻求协调。本节追溯从被动观察到主动"去风险"的轨迹。

4.5.1 Evolution of the Policy Framework

Phase I (2013–2018)

Observation and Engagement

Early on, the EU maintained an observant attitude toward the BRI; certain member states (Greece, Hungary) actively participated, yet the EU level had not formed a unified position.

In 2015, the EU and China signed the EU-China Connectivity Platform Memorandum of Understanding, establishing a policy dialogue mechanism.

早期，欧盟对BRI保持观察态度；某些成员国（希腊、匈牙利）积极参与，但欧盟层面尚未形成统一立场。

2015年，欧盟与中国签署《欧中互联互通平台谅解备忘录》，建立政策对话机制。

Phase II (2019–2021)

Strategic Awakening

As the BRI advanced in Europe, the EU became increasingly aware of its geopolitical implications. The EU FDI Screening Regulation entered into force in 2019, intensifying review of Chinese investment.

In 2021, the EU formally launched the Global Gateway strategy, explicitly positioning it as a "values-based alternative."

随着BRI在欧洲推进，欧盟日益意识到其地缘政治影响。《欧盟外国直接投资审查条例》于2019年生效，加强对华投资审查。

2021年，欧盟正式推出"全球门户"战略，明确将其定位为"基于价值观的替代方案"。

Phase III (2022–Present)

Institutional Competition & "De-risking"

Following the Russia-Ukraine war, the EU accelerated "de-risking" strategy, defining relations with China as "partner, economic competitor, and systemic rival."

In December 2025, the EU issued the new Framework for Strengthening EU Economic Security, proposing six high-risk areas and six "de-risking" policy instruments.

俄乌战争后，欧盟加速"去风险"战略，将中国关系定义为"伙伴、经济竞争者和制度性对手"。

2025年12月，欧盟发布新的《加强欧盟经济安全框架》，提出六大高风险领域和六大"去风险"政策工具。

4.5.2 Specific Responses to the BRI

In practice, the EU's response to the BRI manifests in four strategic instruments that constitute a comprehensive "rule countermeasure" toolkit: 在实践中，欧盟对BRI的回应体现在四种战略工具中，构成全面的"规则反制"工具包：

Defensive

Rule Export

Through Global Gateway, the EU exports its own rules (environmental standards, procurement rules, transparency requirements) as conditions for investment, attempting to establish "EU standards" in the infrastructure domain.

通过全球门户，欧盟输出自身规则（环境标准、采购规则、透明度要求）作为投资条件，试图在基础设施领域建立"欧盟标准"。

Competitive

Standard Competition

Tools such as the Net-Zero Industry Act non-price criteria, Foreign Subsidies Regulation, and FDI screening effectively raise market entry barriers for Chinese enterprises in the EU.

《净零工业法案》非价格标准、《外国补贴条例》和FDI审查等工具有效提高了中国企业在欧盟的市场准入壁垒。

Coordinative

Multilateral Coordination

The EU actively promotes the G7 "Partnership for Global Infrastructure and Investment" (PGII), attempting to form a counterbalance to the BRI by coordinating resources among like-minded countries.

欧盟积极推动G7"全球基础设施与投资伙伴关系"(PGII)，试图通过协调志同道合国家的资源形成对BRI的制衡。

Selective

Selective Cooperation

In certain domains (green finance, climate change), the EU remains willing to maintain dialogue with China. EU-China strategic dialogue on Africa continued during the 2024 FOCAC summit period.

在某些领域（绿色金融、气候变化），欧盟仍愿意与中国保持对话。202年中非合作论坛峰会期间，欧中非洲战略对话继续。

Future Pathway: From Confrontation to Coordination

The contrast between the Piraeus and Budapest–Belgrade cases reveals two possible futures:

  • Piraeus model: When Chinese investors proactively adapt to EU rules and respect local governance, BRI projects can succeed in Europe.
  • Budapest–Belgrade warning: When institutional coordination is insufficient, infrastructure projects become sacrifices to rule competition.

The future direction should seek "rule coordination" rather than "rule confrontation."

比雷埃夫斯与布达佩斯-贝尔格莱德案例的对比揭示了两种可能的未来：

  • 比雷埃夫斯模式：当中国投资者主动适应欧盟规则并尊重当地治理时，BRI项目可以在欧洲取得成功。
  • 布达佩斯-贝尔格莱德警示：当制度协调不足时，基础设施项目成为规则竞争的牺牲品。

未来方向应寻求"规则协调"而非"规则对抗"。

4.6.1 Final Judgment: Institutional Coopetition Confirmed

Two cases, two trajectories. Piraeus, where rules harmonized and the port thrived. Budapest–Belgrade, where rules collided and the project stalled. The contrast is stark—and instructive.

This chapter validates the core thesis: BRI and Global Gateway form an institutional coopetition relationship—competition across dimensions including financing mechanisms, environmental standards, procurement procedures, debt sustainability, transparency, and implementation efficiency; yet potential synergies exist in domains such as green finance, climate change, and third-country market cooperation.

两个案例，两种轨迹。比雷埃夫斯港，规则协调，港口繁荣。布达佩斯-贝尔格莱德，规则碰撞，项目停滞。对比鲜明——且富有教益。

本章验证了核心论点：BRI与全球门户形成制度竞合关系——在融资机制、环境标准、采购程序、债务可持续性、透明度和实施效率等领域竞争；但在绿色金融、气候变化和第三方市场合作等领域存在潜在协同。

Core Theoretical Finding

The variability of rule interaction morphology is not an inherent project attribute but a context-dependent outcome. The same BRI project can exhibit different interaction morphologies across different segments (Serbian section vs. Hungarian section), demonstrating that institutional density of the rule environment is the key moderating variable. 规则互动形态的可变性并非项目的固有属性，而是情境依赖的结果。同一BRI项目在不同路段（塞尔维亚段vs匈牙利段）可展现不同的互动形态，证明制度环境的制度密度是关键调节变量。

Structural Constraints on Arbitrage

The "institutional arbitrage" strategies of Kazakhstan and other countries are feasible in medium-density institutional environments, yet contract sharply in high-density environments (such as Hungary as an EU member). When the number of rule-setting parties exceeds three and rules address core technical standards (rather than peripheral procedural matters), compliance costs exhibit nonlinear multiplication effects—transitioning from "high but controllable" to "cost out of control, project stagnation." 哈萨克斯坦等国的"制度套利"策略在中等制度密度环境中可行，但在高密度环境（如欧盟成员国匈牙利）中急剧收缩。当规则制定方数量超过三个且规则涉及核心技术标准（而非边缘程序事项）时，合规成本呈现非线性乘数效应——从"高但可控"转变为"成本失控、项目停滞"。

Chapter 4 Concluding Statement

First, the Piraeus case validates the feasibility of the BRI "speed advantage" in EU environments, while also demonstrating the practical pathway of Chinese investors proactively adapting to EU rules and bridging the "transparency gap." This case proves that BRI and EU rules are not necessarily contradictory; through respect for local institutions and adherence to commercial operations, mutual benefit and win-win outcomes can be achieved.

Second, the Budapest–Belgrade Railway case highlights the negative effects of rule conflicts. The multiplicity of financing sources, complexity of standard applicability, and uncertainty of EU review have jointly produced severe delays. This case warns that when institutional coordination is insufficient, infrastructure projects may become sacrifices to rule competition.

Third, the contrast between the two cases reveals that rule coordination is the critical prerequisite for BRI success in Europe. Unilateral emphasis on one's own rules while neglecting host-country institutional environments will plunge projects into predicaments; proactive rule adaptation and seeking institutional synergies can achieve mutual benefit and win-win outcomes.

首先，比雷埃夫斯港案例验证了BRI"速度优势"在欧盟环境中的可行性，同时展示了中国投资者主动适应欧盟规则、弥合"透明度鸿沟"的实践路径。该案例证明BRI与欧盟规则并非必然矛盾；通过尊重当地制度和坚持商业运作，可以实现互利共赢。

其次，布达佩斯-贝尔格莱德铁路案例凸显了规则冲突的负面影响。融资来源的多样性、标准适用的复杂性和欧盟审查的不确定性共同导致了严重延误。该案例警示，当制度协调不足时，基础设施项目可能成为规则竞争的牺牲品。

第三，两个案例的对比揭示，规则协调是BRI在欧洲取得成功的关键前提。单方面强调自身规则而忽视东道国制度环境将使项目陷入困境；主动规则适应和寻求制度协同可以实现互利共赢。
