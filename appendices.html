Appendix D: Overview of Primary EU Regulatory Instruments (2024–2025 Update) 附录D：欧盟主要监管工具概述（2024-2025年更新）

This appendix compiles the primary regulatory instruments enacted by the EU in recent years and their potential impacts on BRI projects. 本附录汇编了欧盟近年来颁布的主要监管工具及其对BRI项目的潜在影响。

Instrument Status Core Provisions Impact on Chinese Investors
Foreign Subsidies Regulation (FSR) 2023 effective Reviews acquisitions and public procurement by foreign-subsidized enterprises in EU. By June 2024, 5 investigations launched targeting Chinese enterprises. Must report subsidy situations in public procurement (>€250M threshold). "Foreign subsidies" definition retains broad interpretive space.
International Procurement Instrument (IPI) 2022 effective Authorizes Commission to investigate non-EU procurement market restrictions, potentially limiting access to EU public procurement. Third-country discriminatory measures against EU enterprises trigger reciprocal restrictions in EU public procurement.
Net-Zero Industry Act Non-Price Criteria May 2025 issued; Dec 2025 effective Renewable energy auctions introduce non-price criteria: responsible business conduct, cybersecurity, supply chain resilience, environmental sustainability. China-specific provisions (Art. 7(3)) require 75% non-Chinese origin for wind/electrolyzers. Substantive entry barriers for Chinese photovoltaic, wind power, battery products. Market share >50% or 40% with 10% annual growth triggers mandatory supply chain resilience assessment.
Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD) 2024 adopted, phased implementation Requires enterprises to identify, prevent, mitigate supply chain environmental and social impacts. Applies to non-EU enterprises with global turnover >€450 million. Must establish supply chain due diligence systems; effectively extends EU standards to global supply chains.
Economic Security Strategy Upgrade Dec 2025 issued Proposes six high-risk areas and six "de-risking" policy instruments; strengthens FDI screening, subsidy regulation, procurement review. Chinese enterprises in EU face stricter full-sector review for sensitive technologies and critical infrastructure.
NIS2 Directive (Cybersecurity) 2023 effective Requires critical entities to establish cybersecurity risk management measures; data localization requirements (business data within European Economic Area). Expanded critical entity definition; mandatory "cybersecurity plan" required; vulnerability information disclosure restricted.
FDI Screening Regulation (recast proposed) 2019 effective; 2024 draft revision Reviews foreign direct investments into the Union; 2024 draft expands to greenfield investments and broader screening mechanisms. Screening duration >18 months creates uncertainty; mandatory notification for sensitive sectors.
Key 2024–2025 Dynamics:
  • Expanded review scope: From M&A investments to greenfield investments, from traditional infrastructure to digital, energy, and full supply chain domains.
  • Enhanced targeting: Net-Zero Industry Act Art. 7(3) directly targets Chinese wind power and electrolyzer products.
  • Increased enforcement: FSR investigation cases rising, economic security strategy shifting from "passive defense" to "active shaping."
  • Significant extraterritorial effects: CSDDD extends EU standards to global supply chains.
