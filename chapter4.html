4.0 Case Selection and Research Methods

Theory, in the end, is only as good as its encounter with reality. Chapter 3 laid out a framework; this chapter puts it to the test. Two cases, two outcomes—one success, one failure—each chosen not randomly but with purpose.

Before we step into Piraeus and onto the Budapest–Belgrade line, a word on how and why they were selected.

归根结底，理论的价值取决于其与现实的 encounter。第三章提出了一个框架；本章将对其进行检验。两个案例，两种结果——一个成功，一个失败——每一个都不是随机选择，而是有目的的选择。

在我们进入比雷埃夫斯和布达佩斯-贝尔格莱德铁路之前，先说明它们是如何以及为何被选中的。

4.0.2 Analytical Technique: Theory-Guided Process Tracing

This chapter employs theory-guided process tracing. Evidence standards follow Van Evera's classic typology: Smoking-gun (highly specific), Hoop (basic tests), Straw-in-the-wind (supportive but not decisive). 本章采用理论引导的过程追踪。证据标准遵循Van Evera的经典类型学：确凿证据（高度具体）、圈证据（基本测试）、风中之草（支持性但非决定性）。

4.1 Introduction to the EU's Connectivity Strategy

The European Union's Global Gateway strategy was formally launched in December 2021, constituting the EU's strategic response to global infrastructure investment demands and, explicitly, to the Belt and Road Initiative. The strategy targets the mobilization of €300 billion in investment by 2027.

A defining feature is its values-driven investment philosophy. Unlike the BRI's emphasis on "no political strings attached," the Global Gateway binds investment to principles of democratic values, good governance, transparency, and environmental sustainability.

欧盟的全球门户(Global Gateway)战略于2021年12月正式启动，构成了欧盟对全球基础设施投资需求的战略回应，也明确针对"一带一路"倡议。该战略目标是到2027年动员3000亿欧元的投资。

其决定性特征是价值观驱动的投资理念。与BRI强调"不附加政治条件"不同，全球门户将投资与民主价值观、良好治理、透明度和环境可持续性的原则绑定。

"Five years in, the gap between promise and delivery is hard to ignore. Large-scale cross-border projects remain scarce; disbursements are cautious; attention has drifted toward energy transition at the expense of physical connectivity. The moniker sticks: 'graveyard of hundreds of billions of euros.'" "五年过去了，承诺与交付之间的差距不容忽视。大规模跨境项目仍然稀缺；支出谨慎；注意力已从实体连通性转向能源转型。这个绰号挥之不去：'数千亿欧元的墓地。'"

4.2 Objectives of the EU's Connectivity Strategy

The Global Gateway strategy encompasses five investment domains:

4.2.1 Digital Connectivity

Secure, resilient digital infrastructure. Flagship: MEDUSA cable connecting North Africa to European research networks.

4.2.2 Energy Connectivity

Green transition support. Paradigm case: EU-Namibia partnership integrating port upgrades, electrolyzer manufacturing, and critical raw materials.

4.2.3 Transport Connectivity

Cross-border transport corridors. Flagship: Lobito Corridor (DRC/Zambia copper belt to Angola's Atlantic port).

4.2.4 Health Connectivity

Local vaccine production, pharmaceutical regulatory systems, medical supply chain resilience (post-COVID priority).

4.2.5 Education and Research Connectivity

Youth exchange, higher education cooperation, research network development (Erasmus+ programs).

"Both initiatives talk about transport, energy, and digital infrastructure—the same words, but different meanings. The BRI speaks the language of hardware: roads, ports, railways, power stations. The Global Gateway speaks the language of software: standards, governance, transparency, local capacity building. One builds the machine; the other writes the manual." "两个倡议都谈论交通、能源和数字基础设施——相同的词汇，但不同的含义。BRI讲的是硬件语言：道路、港口、铁路、电站。全球门户讲的是软件语言：标准、治理、透明度、地方能力建设。一个建造机器；另一个编写手册。"

4.3 Case Study 1: The Port of Piraeus—BRI Financial Rules in Practice

Case Outcome: Harmonious Coordination

Adaptive Compliance Success Story

Twenty kilometers southwest of Athens, where the Saronic Gulf meets the Aegean, lies a port that has become an unlikely symbol of China-Europe cooperation. Piraeus is not just Greece's largest port; it is the place where the BRI's ambitions met Europe's regulatory realities—and, against the odds, found a way to coexist. 在雅典西南二十公里处，萨罗尼克湾与爱琴海交汇的地方，有一个港口，它已成为中欧合作的不太可能的象征。比雷埃夫斯不仅是希腊最大的港口；它是BRI的雄心与欧洲监管现实相遇的地方——而且，尽管困难重重，找到了共存的方式。

4.3.7 Latest Developments and Ongoing Compliance Challenges (2024–2025)

The success of the Piraeus project is not permanent; developments in 2024–2025 demonstrate that even after a decade of operation, the project continues to face persistent scrutiny from EU and member state regulatory bodies. 比雷埃夫斯项目的成功并非永久；2024-2025年的发展表明，即使运营了十年，该项目仍面临欧盟和成员国监管机构的持续审查。

February 2026: Pier IV Suspension

The Ethniki Simvoulio Alimenos Limenon (ESAL) approved COSCO's €611.8 million investment plan but suspended the €300 million application for the fourth container terminal, citing "conditions not yet ripe." COSCO's response: "continue communication with authorities" rather than confrontation—demonstrating that adaptive compliance has been internalized as organizational routine.

4.4.3 Rule Interaction Morphology: Formation Mechanisms of Conflictual Confrontation

Analytical Dimension Specific Manifestation Missing Critical Condition
Financing Coordination Failure Five parties unable to agree on rule prioritization; China insists on loan conditions, EU insists on procurement rules Absence of rule arbitration mechanism; no party capable of forced coordination
Technical Standard Conflict Chinese railway standards vs. EU TSI standards; politicized as market access tools; NOBO certification lengthy and costly Absence of technical mutual recognition agreements; lack of China-EU railway standards dialogue
Review Procedure Uncertainty EU investigation launched 2017, persisting years without final conclusion; Hungarian approvals repeatedly delayed Absence of review time limits; investigation remains unconcluded creating expectation confusion

Compliance Cost Stacking Effects

Direct Cost Stacking: Hungarian section EIA required simultaneous satisfaction of China Exim Bank, EU fund, and EBRD formatting requirements—documentation preparation alone took 18 months.
Indirect Cost Multiplication: Project originally planned for 2020 completion, yet by 2026 remained incomplete; 6-year delay period estimated to have increased capital occupancy costs by $200-300 million.

4.4.4 Rule Conflicts and EU Review

EU Commission Investigation (2017)

Launched January 2017, focusing on: (1) project's financial viability; (2) potential violations of EU law requiring "large transport projects to be publicly tendered." If found to violate EU procurement law, project could face fines and prosecution.

Standard Conflict: Chinese vs. EU Standards

Originally planned to adopt Chinese railway technical standards, yet EU requires compliance with Technical Specifications for Interoperability (TSI). Serbian Prime Minister Vučić stated: "Serbia has no obstacles provided they comply with EU standards."

Critical Contrast:

4.4.6 Validation of Theoretical Framework and Refinement

✓ Confirmed Hypotheses

  • Conflictual confrontation arises in contexts of mutually exclusive rules lacking coordination mechanisms
  • Five-party financing generates exponential (not linear) cost multiplication
  • Project delayed 8-9 years with costs estimated increased 50-100%

⚡ Theoretical Refinement

Subdivision of "conflictual confrontation" type:

  • Structural Confrontation: Multi-source financing-induced rule mutual exclusivity (Hungarian section)
  • Procedural Confrontation: Review uncertainty-induced expectation confusion
  • Geopolitically Intensified: External shocks exacerbating existing conflicts (Russian loan factor)

4.4.7 Comparative Summary of Two Cases and Theoretical Implications

Comparative Dimension Piraeus Port (Harmonious Coordination) Budapest–Belgrade (Conflictual Confrontation) Theoretical Implication
Financing Structure Relatively simple (own funds + commercial loans) Highly complex (five parties, divergent rules) Financing structure complexity is a key predictor of rule interaction effects
Institutional Density High (EU member state), yet greater flexibility for commercial projects Gradient variation (Serbia medium-density vs. Hungary high-density) Homogeneity of institutional density matters more than absolute level
Rule Adaptation Strategy Proactive adaptation, three-layer mechanisms Passive response, lack of systematic strategy Proactive vs. passive is the key behavioral variable
Signaling Management Successful coordination of speed and standard signals Signal confusion, declining market confidence Signaling management capacity is a necessary condition for harmonious coordination
Outcome Commercial success, multi-party win-win Severe delays, cost escalation, damage to all parties Rule interaction morphology has causal effects on project performance

Core Theoretical Finding: The same BRI project can exhibit different interaction morphologies across different segments (Serbian vs. Hungarian), demonstrating that rule interaction is not an inherent project attribute but a context-dependent outcome.

4.5 EU's Response to the BRI: Policy Evolution

4.5.1 Evolution of the Policy Framework

2013–2018

Observation and Engagement

EU maintained observant attitude; certain member states (Greece, Hungary) actively participated. 2015: EU-China Connectivity Platform Memorandum of Understanding.

2019–2021

Strategic Awakening

EU FDI Screening Regulation entered into force 2019. 2021: Formal launch of Global Gateway strategy as "values-based alternative."

2022–Present

Institutional Competition and "De-risking"

Post-Ukraine war acceleration. December 2025: New Framework for Strengthening EU Economic Security—six high-risk areas and six "de-risking" policy instruments.

4.5.2 Specific Responses to the BRI

Rule Export

Exporting EU standards as conditions for investment—competing with BRI's "host-country law applicability."

Standard Competition

Net-Zero Industry Act non-price criteria, FSR, FDI screening—raising market entry barriers.

Multilateral Coordination

Promoting G7 "Partnership for Global Infrastructure and Investment" (PGII) as counterbalance.

Selective Cooperation

Maintaining dialogue in green finance and climate change despite competition.

4.6 Chapter Summary

First, the Piraeus case validates the feasibility of the BRI "speed advantage" in EU environments, while also demonstrating the practical pathway of Chinese investors proactively adapting to EU rules and bridging the "transparency gap."

Second, the China–Europe Land–Sea Express Line (Budapest–Belgrade Railway) case highlights the negative effects of rule conflicts. The multiplicity of financing sources, complexity of standard applicability, and uncertainty of EU review have jointly produced severe project delays.

Third, the contrast between the two cases reveals two possible futures for China–Europe connectivity: if both sides persist in rule dialogue and seek standard mutual recognition, the BRI can achieve Piraeus-style success in Europe; if they allow rule competition to proceed without coordination mechanisms, Budapest–Belgrade-style predicaments may be repeated.

首先，比雷埃夫斯案例验证了BRI"速度优势"在欧盟环境中的可行性，同时也展示了中国投资者主动适应欧盟规则、弥合"透明度差距"的实际路径。

其次，中欧陆海快线（布达佩斯-贝尔格莱德铁路）案例凸显了规则冲突的负面影响。融资来源的多样性、标准适用性的复杂性以及欧盟审查的不确定性共同导致了严重的项目延误。

第三，两个案例的对比揭示了中欧互联互通的两种可能未来：如果双方坚持规则对话并寻求标准互认，BRI可以在欧洲取得比雷埃夫斯式的成功；如果任由规则竞争在没有协调机制的情况下发展，布达佩斯-贝尔格莱德式的困境可能会重演。

"The Piraeus model demonstrates that when Chinese investors proactively adapt to EU rules and respect local governance, BRI projects can succeed in Europe and bring win-win outcomes to both sides."
